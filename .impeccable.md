## Design Context

### Users
RoomFlow is used by employees and office administrators inside a company. They need to scan room availability quickly, make reliable bookings, and maintain room readiness during everyday work.

### Brand Personality
Calm, operational, precise. The interface should feel like an internal control surface for repeated daily work rather than a marketing page.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light-first business application with dark mode support. Use restrained color, clear status indicators, dense but readable tables and panels, and practical charts that reveal room utilization without decorative clutter.

### Design Principles
- Keep primary workflows visible: dashboard status, room filtering, booking creation, and personal meetings should be reachable in one step.
- Favor scannable operational layouts over oversized hero sections or promotional composition.
- Make status and conflicts explicit with consistent colors, tags, timelines, and messages.
- Preserve stable dimensions for cards, controls, and lists so data changes do not shift the interface.
- Support dark mode without changing the information architecture.
